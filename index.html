Welcome

It is with great pleasure we welcome you to the 14th meeting of the International Skeletal Dysplasia Society (ISDS). The meeting will be held in Oslo, capital of Norway, from the 11th to the 14th of September 2019. This is the first time the ISDS meeting will be held in Scandinavia. We will do our utmost to offer you a top scientific programme and an enjoyable stay.

Hope to see you all in Oslo.

Yours sincerely Else Merckoll and Cecilie F. Rustad

Venue and Accomodation

The 14th ISDS meeting is in Oslo in 2019 and will be held at the Hotel Bristol (Kristian IVs gate 7, 0164 Oslo). The hotel is situated in the centre of Oslo with very easy access to many tourist attractions and is approximately 750 m from the Nationaltheatret station where the airport express train arrives and departs from.

Hotel Bristol has set aside rooms for ISDS participants. Single room 1895 per night, double room 2195 per night. If you are sharing a double room with another participant, each will be billed 1097,50 per might. We advice you to book accomodation through the ISDS registration and booking of accomodation link below. Travel, visa and practical issues

Oslo is the capital of Norway, home to approximately 673 000 inhabitants and one of the oldest cities in the country. 454km2 it consists of a large forest in the north, east and partly to the west and is by the Oslo fjord. The city is for some well-known for having the Holmenkollen ski festival in March every year and was the host of the Winter Olympic Games in 1952.

At the site Visit Oslo you can find lots of information about the city, restaurants and transport within the city and to and from the city by car, plane and train. You will find information in English, German, Italian, Spanish and French.

Visit Oslo

Perhaps you can combine work with some days of sightseeing? Why not take the opportunity to see more of Norway while you`re here. Norway is a small, but diverse country with magnificent fjords in the west, high mountains with glaciers, woodlands centrally and to the east, picturesque villages along the south coast and a dramatical coastline from Trondheim up to the northernmost point of Europe; the North cape at 71degrees north. Or take a trip to Svalbard to see the arctic island group and reach 78degrees north. We invite you to take a look at Visitnorways website and dream away.

Visa requirements and passports

Please check passport and visa requirements in due time before departure. Visitors from the Schengen countries do not need a passport or visa when entering Norway, but must show valid and sufficient ID. European Union ID-card or passport is recommended.

Countries exempt from visa requirement

A visa is not required for nationals of countries with which Norway has signed a visa waiver agreement. Nationals of countries with a visa waiver may stay in Norway for up to 90 days.

Trains

The airport expresstrain (Flytoget) takes about 20 minutes from the airport to the city centre. Every other train stops at Nationaltheatret station. Leave the train at “Nationaltheatret station” and walk 5-10 min. to Bristol hotel. Address: Kristian IV`s gate 7, 0164 Oslo

If you take the airport express train that stops at Oslo S (Oslo central station) you can either walk a little longer or take any of the westbound tubes (t-bane) to “Nationaltheateret station”. All the westbound lines stop there. You will need another ticket for the tube.

All Vy (formerly NSB Norwegian State Railways) Regional Trains that run between Skien, Oslo, Lillehammer and Trondheim stop at Oslo Airport. Vy Local Trains on the Kongsberg–Eidsvoll line also stop at the Airport.

Public transport within Oslo

All public transport in Oslo and the surrounding county Akershus is part of the same ticket and price system, operated by Ruter. Ruter`s tickets are valid for buses, trams, subways, ferries (not the Bygdøy ferry) and local trains.
