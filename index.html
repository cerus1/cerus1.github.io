Welcome

It is with great pleasure we welcome you to the 14th meeting of the International Skeletal Dysplasia Society (ISDS). The meeting will be held in Oslo, capital of Norway, from the 11th to the 14th of September 2019. This is the first time the ISDS meeting will be held in Scandinavia. In tradition with the previous meetings we will do our utmost to offer you a top scientific program and an enjoyable stay.

Hope to see you all in Oslo.

Yours sincerely Else Merckoll and Cecilie F. Rustad

Programme at a glance

11th of September: Registration at venue, transport to welcome reception to start at around 7 p.m. at Ekebergrestauranten, Kongsveien 15, 0193 Oslo.

12th of September: Scientific sessions at the venue, four sessions with breaks and lunch. Poster session. ISDS general assembly. Optional social program in the evening/free night.

13th of September: Scientific sessions at the venue, four sessions with breaks and lunch. Poster session. Transportation to conference dinner at Roklubben, 2B, Huk Aveny, 0287 Oslo, with museum visit on the way.

14th of September: Two scientific sessions. Award ceremony. Closing remarks and lunch.

Venue and Accomodation

Venue and accommodation Venue: The ISDS 2019 14th Oslo meeting will be held at the Hotel Bristol (Kristian IVs gate 7, 0164 Oslo). The hotel is situated in the centre of Oslo with very easy access to many tourist attractions and is approximately 750 m from the Nationaltheateret station where the airport express train arrives and departs from. Accommodation: Hotel Bristol has set aside rooms at a lower rate for congress participants: 1895 NOK per room including breakfast.

Travel, visa and practical issues

Oslo is the capital of Norway and one of the oldest cities in Norway. With approximately 673 000 inhabitants, sitting by the see of the Oslo fjord and stretching out 454km2 it also consists of a large forest in the north, east and partly to the west. The city is for some well-known for having the Holmenkollen ski festival in March every year and was the host of the Winter Olympic Games in 1952.

At the site Visit Oslo you can find lots of information about the city, restaurants and transport within the city and to and from the city by car, plane and train. You will find information in English, German, Italian, Spanish and French.

www.visitoslo.com

Perhaps you can combine work with some days of sightseeing? Why not take the opportunity to see more of Norway, too while you`re here. Norway is a small, but diverse country with magnificent fjords in the west, high mountains with glaciers, woodlands centrally and to the east, picturesque villages along the south coast and a dramatical coastline from Trondheim up to the northernmost point of Europe; the North cape at 71degrees north. Or take a trip to Svalbard to see the arctic island group and reach 78degrees north. We invite you to take a look at Visitnorways website and dream away.

Travel information to and from the main airport; Oslo Airport Gardermoen: https://avinor.no/en/airport/to-and-from-the-airport/train-buss-and-taxi/ Trains The train takes about 20 minutes from the city centre to the airport. Airport Express Train (Flytoget) The Airport Express Train runs between Drammen, Asker, Sandvika, Lysaker, Skøyen, Nationaltheatret, Oslo S and Lillestrøm. NSB – Norwegian State Railways All NSB Regional Trains that run between Skien, Oslo, Lillehammer and Trondheim stop at Oslo Airport. NSB Local Trains on the Kongsberg–Eidsvoll line also stop at the Airport. Leave the arrivals area by turning right at the exit gates; take the escalator or stairs down to the platform. By the ticket from the ticket machine upstairs before you go down or draw your credit card as you go through the barriers. Every other train stops at Nationalteateret station. Leave the train at “Nationalteateret station” and walk 5-10 min. to Bristol hotel. If you take the airport exprres train that stops at Oslo S (Oslo central station) you can either walk a little longer or take any of the westbound tubes (t-bane) to “Nationalteateret station”. All the westbound lines stop there. You will need another ticket for the tube. Buses: Ruter Ruter buses run to and from Oslo Airport and make a number of stops in the vicinity of the Airport. *Airport Express Coach (SAS Flybussen) The Airport Express Coach runs between Oslo city centre and Oslo Airport every 20 minutes every day. *Nor-way bus Airport Coach (Flybussekspressen) The Nor-way bus Airport Coach runs to and from Majorstuen, Bekkestua, Ski and Fredrikstad. Nettbuss Nettbuss runs services at Oslo Airport. Nettbuss also runs shuttle bus services between the airport and nearby hotels. Taxi If you want to take a taxi from the airport, we recommend you go to the taxi information desk in the arrivals hall. The taxi information desk has information about the fixed rates of the different companies that operate out of Oslo Airport. They can also book a taxi for you. The taxi rank is immediately outside the arrivals hall. You can usually find a taxi from most taxi companies there. Public transport within Oslo

All public transport in Oslo and the surrounding county Akershus is part of the same ticket and price system, operated by Ruter. Ruter`s tickets are valid for buses, trams, subways, ferries (not the Bygdøy ferry) and local trains.
